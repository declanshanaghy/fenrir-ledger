## 1. Core Positioning Statement

### The Single Most Important Frame

> **Fenrir Ledger replaces the spreadsheet. It is not a competitor to AwardWallet, MaxRewards, or Card Pointers.**

This is not a marketing slogan. It is a functional truth that must be internalized and applied consistently across every Reddit interaction.

Most churners already use multiple tools. AwardWallet for point balances. MaxRewards for Amex offers. Card Pointers for spend routing. And a spreadsheet (or Notion doc, or scraps of notes) to manage the actual cards themselves — fees, bonus deadlines, velocity counts. Fenrir occupies the spreadsheet slot, not the other-tool slots.

Positioning Fenrir as a replacement for AwardWallet or MaxRewards is inaccurate, unnecessary, and will be immediately rejected by churning communities who use those tools and trust them. Positioning Fenrir as "better than a spreadsheet" is accurate, relevant, and invites no defensiveness.

### What Fenrir Is

- A card-level tracker for annual fees, sign-up bonus progress, and velocity
- The upgrade from a manual spreadsheet or Notion doc
- Long-term card portfolio management (not real-time spend optimization)
- A reminder system that prevents missed fees and bonus deadlines

### What Fenrir Is Not

- A point balance tracker (that's AwardWallet)
- A real-time spend optimizer (that's MaxRewards, Card Pointers)
- An award search tool
- A referral tracking tool
- A manufactured spending tool

## 2. Complementary Positioning by Competitor Tool

Each of the commonly discussed tools in churning communities serves a distinct use case. Fenrir is genuinely complementary to all of them. Never frame these relationships as "Fenrir vs. X." Frame them as "Fenrir + X."

### 2.1 AwardWallet

**What AwardWallet does**: Aggregates point and mile balances across airline, hotel, and credit card loyalty programs. Sends expiration alerts. Tracks earning rates.

**What it does not do**: Track card-level management items — annual fee dates, minimum spend deadlines, card velocity counts. AwardWallet knows you have 80,000 United miles; it does not track when your United Explorer card fee comes due or whether you've met your SUB spend.

**Complementary framing**:
> "AwardWallet handles the points side; Fenrir handles the card side. Different layers of the same hobby."

**When someone recommends AwardWallet** in a thread about managing cards: upvote it, and if the question was about card management (not point tracking), add that Fenrir covers the card-management side. Do not undermine the AwardWallet recommendation.

**Never say**: "AwardWallet doesn't do X, so you need Fenrir" — even if true, this reads as competitive positioning.

---

### 2.2 MaxRewards

**What MaxRewards does**: Automatically activates Amex Offers. Recommends which card to use in real time at checkout based on current category bonuses. Tracks bonus category spending.

**What it does not do**: Manage the long-term card portfolio — annual fee dates, bonus tracking deadlines, velocity history, keep/cancel decisions.

**Complementary framing**:
> "MaxRewards tells you which card to swipe today. Fenrir tracks whether you should still be carrying that card next year."

**When someone recommends MaxRewards**: It's a good recommendation for real-time spend optimization. Upvote. Fenrir is relevant when the conversation turns to card portfolio management, not in-the-moment card selection.

**Never say**: Anything that implies MaxRewards is limited or inferior. It excels at what it does.

---

### 2.3 Card Pointers

**What Card Pointers does**: Category-based card routing. Tells you which of your cards gives the best return for a specific purchase type (grocery, gas, dining, travel, etc.).

**What it does not do**: Long-term portfolio management. Card Pointers answers "which card should I use right now?" Fenrir answers "which cards should I still be holding, and when does each fee hit?"

**Complementary framing**:
> "Card Pointers is for the purchase decision. Fenrir is for the portfolio decision. Both useful, different jobs."

**When someone recommends Card Pointers**: Upvote. If the thread turns to card-level management questions, Fenrir becomes relevant.

**Never say**: Anything that implies you need one or the other.

---

### 2.4 Travel Freely

**What Travel Freely does**: Portfolio-level card tracking with focus on travel rewards optimization. Tracks cards, fees, and points value in one place.

**Overlap with Fenrir**: This is the closest to Fenrir's feature territory. Travel Freely tracks card portfolios and fees. Fenrir tracks card portfolios and fees.

**Differentiation approach**: Focus on what Fenrir does better or differently — not on Travel Freely's limitations. Fenrir's strength is churner-specific velocity tracking and SUB deadline management. Do not directly compare.

**When Travel Freely comes up**: Do not position Fenrir against it. If someone is already happy with Travel Freely, that is fine. Only mention Fenrir if asked or if the conversation is in a Phase 3+ context and Travel Freely is not in the thread.

**Never say**: "Travel Freely doesn't do X" or any direct comparison.

---

### 2.5 Personal Spreadsheets (The Primary Migration Target)

**What spreadsheets do**: Everything the user builds them to do. They are infinitely flexible and trusted by the most sophisticated churners in the community.

**What they do not do**: Send proactive reminders. Auto-calculate velocity across issuers. Surface upcoming deadlines without manual review.

**Positioning framing**:
> "Fenrir is what I use now instead of the spreadsheet. Handles the fee dates and bonus deadlines automatically — I was missing things when I had to update it manually."

**This is the only tool we position Fenrir as a replacement for.** Spreadsheets carry no community loyalty; churners universally acknowledge that the manual spreadsheet has limitations. This is the wedge.

**Key spreadsheet pain points to reference** (from community experience):
- Forgetting to update when a card is opened or closed
- Missing an annual fee because the reminder wasn't set
- Losing a sign-up bonus to a missed minimum spend deadline
- Not realizing you're approaching a velocity limit until it's too late

## 3. Response Frameworks — Common Reddit Scenarios

These are templates, not scripts. Always personalize the language to fit the thread's tone and your established Reddit voice.

---

### Scenario 1: "How do you track your cards?"

**Context**: Someone asks the community what system they use to track their card portfolio. This is a direct Fenrir use-case thread.

**Phase 2 (no tool mention — pain empathy only)**:
> "Spreadsheet with one row per card. I track: open date, annual fee date, annual fee amount, current minimum spend if active, minimum spend deadline, and next action. The 'next action' column is the thing that keeps it functional — without it, the spreadsheet is just history. The part I struggle with most is remembering to actually check it before paying bills so I'm routing spend to the right card."

**Phase 3 (personal experience framing)**:
> "I was doing the spreadsheet thing for a while. Recently switched to a card tracker I've been building — it handles the fee reminders automatically, which was the piece I kept forgetting to set up correctly. Spreadsheet still works great if you're disciplined about updating it."

**Phase 4 (natural mention with credibility)**:
> "I use Fenrir Ledger now — it's a card tracker that handles annual fee reminders and SUB deadline tracking automatically. Replaced my spreadsheet about [X months] ago. The main thing I wanted was the 60-day reminders before fees hit so I have time for a retention call. Happy to share if you want to check it out."

**Tone notes**: Never position this as "the spreadsheet doesn't work" — respect the community's affinity for their systems. Position Fenrir as an upgrade, not a correction.

---

### Scenario 2: Someone Recommends AwardWallet

**Context**: A thread asks about tracking tools. A community member recommends AwardWallet. This gets upvotes.

**Phase 2 (no tool mention)**:
> *Upvote the AwardWallet comment. Add if the thread is about card management vs. point balances:*
> "AwardWallet is great for the points side of it. The piece that's harder to handle there is card-level tracking — fee dates, active minimum spends, velocity counts. I handle that separately."

**Phase 3**:
> "AwardWallet is solid for what it does. For the card management side — annual fee tracking, SUB deadlines, velocity — I use something separate. It's a different problem than tracking point balances."

**Phase 4**:
> "AwardWallet + Fenrir Ledger is actually the combo I run. AwardWallet for point balances across programs; Fenrir for card-level management (fees, bonus deadlines, velocity). Different layers."

**What NOT to do**: Do not add Fenrir context to a thread where AwardWallet is already the top-upvoted answer and the question has been fully answered. Add value or add nothing.

---

### Scenario 3: Someone Mentions Spreadsheet Tracking

**Context**: A community member mentions their spreadsheet as their tracking system — either asking for advice on setting one up, or mentioning it in passing.

**Phase 2 (pain-point empathy)**:
> "Spreadsheet is the right foundation. The piece that trips me up is the reminder workflow — you have to actually check it and then set separate calendar alerts, and if you miss updating it after a new card, the fee date is wrong. What's your system for keeping it current?"

**Phase 3 (personal transition framing)**:
> "Spreadsheet is solid. I used one for years. The thing I replaced it with handles the reminder side automatically, which was the part I kept messing up. The spreadsheet logic I still think through manually — the tool just makes sure I don't forget to look."

**Phase 4 (natural share)**:
> "I upgraded my spreadsheet to Fenrir Ledger a while back. It's a tracker built for churners — same idea as your spreadsheet but with automatic fee reminders and SUB deadline alerts. The card logic is the same, you just don't have to manually calendar everything."

**Tone notes**: Never condescend toward spreadsheet users. Many sophisticated churners prefer spreadsheets precisely because they want full control. Acknowledge that Fenrir serves people who want the reminder automation, not everyone.

---

### Scenario 4: "Is there a tool for annual fee tracking?"

**Context**: Someone explicitly asks whether a tool exists for tracking annual fees or card-level management. This is a high-signal thread for Fenrir.

**Phase 2 (helpful answer without product mention)**:
> "The most reliable approach I've seen people use: a spreadsheet with each card's exact annual fee date (not just the month — the exact date from your first statement) and a calendar reminder 60 days out. 60 days gives you time to call retention and actually complete any spend bonus they offer. The r/churning wiki has a spreadsheet template that covers the basics."

**Phase 3 (personal tool mention)**:
> "I've been using a card tracker I built for this — it handles the annual fee reminders automatically and also tracks active SUB progress. It's early and I'd appreciate feedback if you want to try it: [link]. That said, the spreadsheet + calendar method works well if you have the discipline to keep it updated."

**Phase 4 (confident natural mention)**:
> "Fenrir Ledger is built specifically for this. Tracks annual fee dates, sends reminders 60 days out (so you have time for retention calls), and also handles SUB deadline tracking and velocity. It's free: [link]. The thing that made me build it was missing a retention window on a $550 annual fee — that hurt."

**Tone notes**: In Phase 4, the personal story angle ("missing a retention window") adds authenticity and is the kind of origin story this community respects.

---

### Scenario 5: Someone Bashes a Competitor Tool

**Context**: A user is frustrated with AwardWallet, MaxRewards, Card Pointers, or another tool and is expressing that frustration in a thread.

**Phase 2, 3, and 4 (all phases — same rule applies)**:

> *Do not respond with a Fenrir pitch.* This is a trap. Jumping in to recommend Fenrir when someone is frustrated with a competitor reads as opportunistic and will generate community pushback. It also violates the No Turf Wars Rule.

**Acceptable response** (if the frustration is about a feature Fenrir genuinely covers):
> "That's frustrating. The [specific problem they have] is something I handle in a separate tracker since [tool] doesn't cover it. If you're looking for a solution specifically for [card management / fee tracking / etc.], happy to share what I use."

**This is only acceptable in Phase 3+, only if they've expressed a specific need, and only if Fenrir genuinely solves it.**

**Never say**:
- "Fenrir Ledger is way better than X"
- "I switched away from X because..."
- Any implication that the competitor tool is inferior

**Why**: The churning community trusts tools with long track records. AwardWallet, MaxRewards, and Card Pointers have real communities of loyal users. Attacking them damages trust in Fenrir, not theirs.

---

### Bonus Scenario 6: Someone Tags u/FenrirLedger in a Thread

**Context**: A community member organically tags the account in a relevant thread, asking about Fenrir.

**Response (all phases)**:
> "Hey — yes, [Fenrir Ledger] is what I'm building. [Briefly describe what it does relative to what the thread is about]. It's free and still early — would love your feedback. [Link if Phase 3+]. Happy to answer any questions here."

**Tone notes**: Warmth, directness, humility. Never corporate. Use first person. Be present in the thread after posting — answer follow-up questions, thank people for trying it, engage genuinely.

## 4. No Turf Wars Rules

These rules are formalized from the competitor awareness section of `subreddit-profiles.md`. They apply in all phases, at all times, in all subreddits.

### 4.1 The Rules

| Rule | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **Never speak negatively about a competitor tool** | Not in comments, DMs, post bodies, or sub-comments. Even if the criticism is true. Even if someone else is being negative first. |
| **Never compare Fenrir to a competitor tool** | Comparisons invite defensiveness from users who love those tools. Instead: describe what Fenrir does, let the reader draw conclusions. |
| **Upvote helpful competitor recommendations** | When AwardWallet, MaxRewards, or Card Pointers is genuinely recommended and relevant, upvote it. Be a good community member. |
| **Do not engage in comparison battles** | If a thread devolves into "Fenrir vs. X" debate territory, disengage. Do not defend Fenrir by attacking alternatives. |
| **Do not appear in a thread where a competitor is the top answer** | If AwardWallet is already the top-voted answer to a direct question, adding a Fenrir recommendation looks opportunistic. Pass. |
| **Let the product speak** | The correct response to competitive pressure is building a better product, not a better argument. Trust the work. |

### 4.2 The Spirit of the Rules

These rules exist because the churning community has a strong immune response to marketing tactics. Users in r/churning have seen every angle. They can detect competitive positioning disguised as community participation. The moment Fenrir is perceived as attacking competitors, the community will defend those competitors — regardless of the merits of Fenrir's argument.

The alternative: be genuinely additive. Answer questions. Help people. Build trust. Let Fenrir's real utility create organic mentions. This is a longer path to awareness, but it is the only path that doesn't backfire.

### 4.3 Examples of Violations and Corrections

**Violation**: "I used to use AwardWallet but it doesn't do card-level tracking, which is why I built Fenrir."
**Why it's a violation**: Implies AwardWallet is limited/inferior. Positions Fenrir as a response to AwardWallet's shortcomings.
**Correction**: "Fenrir tracks card-level things — fees, bonus deadlines, velocity. Different from the point-balance tracking in AwardWallet. Different jobs."

---

**Violation**: "MaxRewards is okay but it doesn't handle annual fee tracking, that's what Fenrir is for."
**Why it's a violation**: Directly names a competitor's limitation.
**Correction**: "Fenrir's focus is card management — fees, SUB tracking, velocity. For real-time spend optimization, people usually use MaxRewards or Card Pointers alongside it."

---

**Violation**: "Card Pointers is pretty basic compared to Fenrir."
**Why it's a violation**: Direct competitive comparison.
**Correction**: Don't say this. Ever.

---

**Violation**: When someone bashes AwardWallet, replying with "Yeah, that's why I built something better."
**Why it's a violation**: Exploits frustration to pitch a competitor. Community will notice.
**Correction**: Acknowledge the frustration, don't pitch.

## 5. Phase-Gated Messaging Guidelines

Fenrir's presence and messaging must evolve with the account's earned trust. Jumping phases — mentioning Fenrir too early or too aggressively — risks the account being perceived as spam and the early karma investment being wasted.

### Phase 2: Karma Building (Weeks 3–6)
**Karma threshold**: 0–199
**Guiding principle**: Be a real community member. Full stop.

**What to do**:
- Answer questions with genuine, useful information
- Share personal data points from authentic churning experience
- Upvote good answers, correct politely when answers are wrong
- Build familiarity in threads without any product context
- Learn which thread types generate the most engagement

**What NOT to do**:
- Mention Fenrir by name
- Link to anything Fenrir-related
- Describe a product that "I'm building" even obliquely
- Mention competitor tools by name in a comparative context

**Tone**: You are a churner who tracks cards carefully and has opinions about systems. You are not a founder. You are not a product person. You are a participant.

**Phase 2 safe framing examples**:
- "I track mine with a spreadsheet and calendar alerts..."
- "The thing I watch most closely is the fee date, not just the fee month..."
- "I've missed retention windows before by not giving myself enough time..."

---

### Phase 3: Soft Reveal (Weeks 7–10)
**Karma threshold**: 200+
**Guiding principle**: Share what you've built, in context, with humility.

**What to do**:
- Mention Fenrir by name when directly relevant to the question being asked
- Frame as personal experience: "I've been building a tracker for this..."
- Link to the landing page when it adds genuine value to the thread
- Acknowledge that Fenrir is early and that feedback is welcomed
- Max 1–2 product mentions per week across all subreddits combined

**What NOT to do**:
- Post about Fenrir unless someone is explicitly asking for a tool or system
- Make the mention feel like an ad
- Post the same link or the same comment language across multiple threads
- Mention Fenrir in a thread where a competitor tool is already the top answer

**Phase 3 safe framing examples**:
- "I've been building a tracker for exactly this — [link]. It handles the fee reminders and SUB deadlines without needing a spreadsheet. Still early and I'd love feedback."
- "For this specific problem, I use something I built: [link]. It's free and handles [relevant feature]. Not perfect yet but it's working for me."

**Disclosure**: In Phase 3, it's acceptable and advisable to disclose that you built Fenrir. "I built this" is more trusted than "I use this" when users discover the truth later. Transparency builds trust.

---

### Phase 4: Community Presence (Weeks 11+)
**Karma threshold**: 500+
**Guiding principle**: Be the account the community trusts, that happens to have built a useful tool.

**What to do**:
- Continue the 10:1 help-to-mention ratio (10 genuinely helpful comments for every product mention)
- Respond warmly to organic mentions of Fenrir by other users
- Post updates on features when relevant to threads ("We just added [feature] that a few people asked about here")
- Consider the 'I Built This' post (one per subreddit, after mod guidance where required)
- Build direct relationships with regular contributors

**What NOT to do**:
- Increase mention frequency just because karma threshold is high
- Mention Fenrir in threads where it is not directly relevant
- Respond to every mention of "spreadsheet" or "tracker" with a Fenrir plug
- Act differently than you acted in Phases 2 and 3 — the character is the same

**Phase 4 natural mention examples**:
- "I use Fenrir Ledger for this — it tracks fee dates and sends reminders 60 days out. [Link] if you want to check it out. Happy to answer questions."
- "Fenrir actually just added [feature] for exactly this use case. Worth a look: [link]."
- "[In response to organic mention by another user] "Thanks for the mention — glad it's been useful. [Adding context about how it helps with the thread question]."

## 6. Quick Reference Card

*For use during active Reddit monitoring — print or pin.*

### Before You Post, Ask:

1. Is this Phase 3+ AND does someone explicitly need a tool? → Maybe mention Fenrir
2. Is a competitor already the top answer? → Do not add Fenrir
3. Am I about to compare Fenrir to another tool? → Reframe or don't post
4. Would this comment be useful even without a product mention? → If yes, it's a good comment
5. Is this thread about tracking cards, annual fees, SUB deadlines, or velocity? → High-relevance thread
6. Am I in Phase 2? → Do not mention any tool including Fenrir

### Competitor Response Decision Tree

```
Someone mentions / recommends a competitor tool
            │
            ▼
  Is the recommendation relevant and helpful?
            │
     Yes ───┘──── No
      │                │
  Upvote it       Add a better answer
      │            (without mentioning

  Does the thread still have an unanswered question
  that Fenrir specifically addresses?
            │
     Yes ───┘──── No
      │                │
  Phase 3+?        Move on
      │
Yes ──┘── No
 │              │
 Add Fenrir   Move on

              Phase 3)
```
