Questions About the Price

Everything you'd want to know before deciding.

Is Thrall actually free forever? +

Yes. The Thrall tier has no time limit, no trial expiry, and no credit card required. Every core tracking feature — annual fees, sign-up bonuses, velocity management, The Howl — is free. We don't reduce functionality over time to push upgrades.

What's the difference between Thrall and Karl in practice? +

Thrall covers everything you need to track cards on a single device. Karl adds the features that matter when you're serious: your data syncs everywhere you're signed in, you can manage cards for multiple people in your household, and you can import from a spreadsheet or export at any time. If you only track your own cards on one device, Thrall may be all you need.

How does billing work? +

Karl subscriptions are billed monthly via Stripe. You can cancel anytime from your account settings — your subscription stays active until the end of the current billing period. We don't offer annual billing at this time.

What happens to my data if I cancel Karl? +

Your card data stays intact. If you were using Cloud Sync, your cards remain in the cloud and continue to be accessible — but new changes will only sync after re-subscribing. Multi-Household cards are preserved but you'll only be able to view your primary household until you re-subscribe. We never delete your data when you downgrade.

Why $3.99 and not free with ads? +

Fenrir Ledger tracks sensitive financial data — card names, fee amounts, application history. Ads mean ad networks. Ad networks mean your data leaves this site. That's not a trade we're willing to make. $3.99/month keeps Fenrir independent and your data private.

Does Smart Import store my spreadsheet data? +

Smart Import sends your spreadsheet to an AI model to extract card data. The spreadsheet content is processed in memory and not stored after extraction. Only the extracted card records — name, dates, amounts — are saved to your ledger.

Do you offer refunds? +

If something is wrong, contact us at support@fenrirledger.com within 7 days of a charge and we'll make it right. We don't have a formal refund policy because we haven't needed one.

Interaction Spec — Pricing Page:

Tier cards: Side-by-side on desktop (>768px), stacked on mobile. Karl card has a "Most Popular" badge, 2px border (vs 1px for Thrall), and primary background CTA button. Thrall has ghost/outline CTA.

Feature list in tier cards: Excluded features use strikethrough text + low opacity. This communicates what Thrall doesn't have without being punitive. No lock icons — they feel hostile on a pricing page.

Comparison table: Static table, no interactive filtering. Category rows use muted background (bg-muted) to group features visually. ✓ uses primary color. — (em dash) uses muted/low-opacity foreground.

FAQ accordion: Client component — expand/collapse on click. All items collapsed by default. Single open item at a time (accordion behavior). The + icon rotates to × when open. Uses FaqAccordion component pattern from /faq page (or a simplified inline version if the pricing FAQ is small enough).

PREMIUM_FEATURES registry: Feature gates in the comparison table match the PremiumFeature slugs in types.ts: cloud-sync, multi-household, data-export, (smart-import maps to a future slug). Do not list advanced-analytics, extended-history, or cosmetic-perks — these are not yet exposed in the product per the issue spec.

CTAs: "Start Free →" links to /app (app sign-in/sign-up flow). "Upgrade to Karl →" also links to /app — the app handles subscription flow via Stripe. Do not link directly to Stripe Checkout from the marketing page.

Light/dark: Tier cards use bg-card border-border. Featured Karl card adds a ring/border in primary or foreground color. All semantic tokens. Comparison table alternates row bg between transparent and bg-muted/5.
