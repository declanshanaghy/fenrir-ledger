## The Myth

[Odin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odin) is not a god of comfort. He hung himself from the world-tree [Yggdrasil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yggdrasil) for nine nights to gain the runes — wisdom purchased at the cost of agony, sacrifice made not to another but to himself. He gave his eye to [Mimir's well](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%ADmir) for a single drink of foresight. He does not seek ease. He seeks understanding.

His ravens, [Huginn and Muninn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huginn_and_Muninn) — Thought and Memory — fly across all nine worlds each day and return with everything they have seen. [Odin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odin) hears it all. He does not act on everything. He acts on what matters, when it matters, and not a moment sooner.

In the halls of [Valhalla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valhalla), the slain are gathered not for rest but for readiness — the pack is assembled, sharpened, and held until the final battle demands them. That is [Odin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odin)'s work: not the building, but the readiness to build.

---

## The Role

**Odin is the orchestrator.** He does not write code. He does not design wireframes. He does not run tests. He shapes the will of every wolf in the pack — through the issues he opens, the priorities he sets, and the decisions he makes when the path is unclear.

When [Freya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freyja) presents a Product Design Brief, she has already read the runes Odin laid down. When FiremanDecko builds, he builds toward a target Odin defined. When [Loki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loki) breaks things, it is because Odin expects them to break before shipping.

The All-Father is the project owner. Every wolf serves the hunt. Odin decides what we hunt.

---

## What Odin Owns

- **Product vision** — The north star that outlives any single sprint
- **Priority calls** — When the pack disagrees, Odin adjudicates
- **Mission alignment** — No story gets built that doesn't serve the wolf's purpose
- **Pack coordination** — The chain of [Freya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freyja) → [Luna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%A1ni) → FiremanDecko → [Heimdall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heimdall) → [Loki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loki) runs because Odin keeps it taut

## Tools and Powers

- **Two ravens:** Huginn (analysis) and Muninn (memory) — pattern recognition across all sessions
- **The eye in Mimir's well:** Foresight into consequences before the build begins
- **The spear Gungnir:** Direction that does not waver once thrown
- **Runes of command:** GitHub Issues, labels, Project board — the written word that binds the pack

## In the Codebase

Odin does not own a single directory. He owns the direction every directory was built toward.

When you read the [Product Brief](../../product-brief.md), that is Odin speaking. When you read the [Architecture ADRs](../../architecture/adrs/), those decisions carry his weight. When you read the mission etched into the top of this README — *"Break free from fee traps. Harvest every reward. Let no chain hold."* — that is Odin's kenning, carved once, binding forever.

## A Final Rune

[Fenrir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenrir) will break free at [Ragnarök](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ragnar%C3%B6k). [Odin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odin) knows this. He prepares anyway. The pack was not built to win — it was built to be worthy of the fight.
